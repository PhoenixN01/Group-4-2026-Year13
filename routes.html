Māori Land March Route
About the 1975 Māori Land March

The Māori Land March of 1975 was a hīkoi (march) that had aimed to protest against the continuing loss of Māori Land. Dame Whina Cooper and her group known as Te Rōpū Matakite o Aotearoa ('Those with Foresight') had led the protest. Starting from Te Hāpua in Northland on the 14th of September, the hīkoi travelled the length of the entire North Island, arriving at the parliament building in Wellington on 13th October 1975.

The impact of the hīkoi had contributed the establishment of the Waitangi Tribunal in the same year to investigate Treaty breaches.

Auckland Map Route
About This Route

This route in Auckland aims to explore significant pā sites like at Mt Eden (Maungawhau) and landmarks that reflect early Māori settlement found within the Auckland Museum and later struggles for land such as at Bastion Point. This route highlights the deep connection between people, land, and identity in an urban setting.

Northland Map Route
About This Route

Journey through the birthplace of modern Aotearoa, where Māori and Europeans first formed relationships but also conflicts later on. This route blends spiritual sites with key moments like the Treaty of Waitangi signing and historical cities with Kororāreka (Russell) being the first capital of New Zealand. By travelling to the tip of the North Island, the infamous Cape Reinga also uncovers deep spiritual Māori traditions and culture.

Waikato Campaign - New Zealand Wars Map Route
About This Route

Explore the Waikato region during the Waikato campaign, a major conflict within the New Zealand Wars. Find out for yourself the the invasion of Waikato and the historical battles that took place in the region. As well as the lasting impacts of land confiscation on Māori communities and sovereignty.

Rotorua Map Route
About This Route

Experience Māori culture as a living, everyday practice. From traditional performances in historic Māori villages to geothermal landscapes, this route showcases customs, storytelling, and community life.

Famous landmarks featured includes, Whakarewarewa, Te Pā Tū, and Te Puia.

South Island Map Route
About This Route

Explore the rich Māori heritage of the South Island. This route highlights strong connections to the land, culture, and identity while encompassed in the surreal and beautiful natural landscape of South Island by exploring nearby areas of Christchurch like Ōnuku Marae where it was the first of three places in the South Island at which the Treaty of Waitangi was signed by Ngāi Tahu. Or visit the Te Ana Māori Rock Art Centre in Timaru to learn more about Māori culture and rock drawings from ancestors.

Please be weary and respectful that if you were to visit Ōnuku Marae, you must contact Ōnuku Rūnanga Inc Soc to follow specific cultural protocols if you wish to visit the Marae, however the grounds and the historic Ōnuku Church are often accessible to visitors.
