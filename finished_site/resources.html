Trusted Information Sources

Here are a few trusted sources that you can visit to learn more about Māori Culture from learning new words and phrases to exploring the many different stories that New Zealand has to tell

Te Ara: Māori Encyclopedia

Te Ara is a trusted New Zealand source that provides a collection of Māori Stories and tales about our History and Culture. These stories contain all you wish to know about our History on New Zealand, from the beginning of our journey to the world that you see today.

Visit Te Ara

Te Aka: Māori Dictionary

Te Aka is a Māori Dictionary that provides translation both from Māori to English and English to Māori. This trusted source is used by New Zealanders all across the country, providing access to both translation and context examples for when to use words and how.

Visit Te Aka

Britannica: Encyclopedia

Britannica is another Encyclopedia that contains articles about Māori History and their language. This trusted source is used worldwide, providing a place for many cultures around the world including Māori to be shared and have their stories discovered by people with all levels of experience.

Visit Britannica

NZ History

NZHistory is a trusted source provided by the government that holds information focusing purely on the history of Māori. This website can be especially helpful for people who wish to avoid all of the extra details and focus on the key events that changed the course of Māori Culture.

Visit NZHistory
